About Me

My name is David Rodriguez, and I was born in the vibrant city of Dallas, TX, in April 2002. At the tender age of three, my entire family embarked on a journey, relocating to Michoacán, Mexico. This beautiful place, with its rich history and culture, is where I spent my formative years, building memories and experiences that I hold dear.

It was around the age of eleven that I discovered a fascination with the digital world. I found myself intrigued by computers, the vast field of cybersecurity, and the art of programming. The idea of creating solutions through lines of code captivated me, and even at that young age, I knew that my professional future would revolve around computers.

Life took another turn when, at the age of fifteen, I found myself returning to Texas. My educational journey led me to North Garland High School, where I stayed for two months before my family moved once again, this time to Colorado. It was during this chapter of my life in the beautiful state of Colorado that my interest in programming took a stronger hold on me.

I fell in love with the field of programming for its ability to provide countless avenues for creative expression. Much like an artist finds solace in their canvas, I found mine in the realm of code. After my sophomore year, I relocated once again to Garland, TX, where I concluded my high school journey, graduating from Garland High School with the Class of 2020. With high school behind me, I set foot on a new adventure—college. Currently, I am pursuing an Associate of Applied Science (AAS) degree in Software Development, taking one step closer towards my dreams each day.

Important Fact about Janitzio Michoacan Mexico

Janitzio, a small island located in the heart of Lake Pátzcuaro, Michoacán, Mexico, holds a profound cultural significance, particularly during the Día de los Muertos (Day of the Dead) celebrations. Known for its vibrant, mystical, and awe-inspiring customs, Janitzio's Day of the Dead observances are among the most renowned in the country, attracting numerous domestic and international visitors. On this occasion, the island community showcases an enchanting spectacle as they paddle their boats adorned with candles and marigold flowers over the lake to the local cemetery, where graves are decorated in honor of the departed. With prayers, music, and the sharing of food, this ethereal nocturnal celebration not only symbolizes the reunion of the living with the deceased but also represents a meaningful display of Mexico's rich cultural heritage and indigenous roots.

I love Janitzio Island because of its welcoming habitants and the unique tranquility it maintains throughout the year. Despite its status as a prominent tourist attraction, the island retains a serene ambiance, offering a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of daily life, which makes it even more endearing to me.

This library has equipped me with the tools to stay on the cutting edge of design trends.
